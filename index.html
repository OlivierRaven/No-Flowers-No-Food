🌼 No Flowers = No Food 🌼

New Zealand’s and the wolds insects and pollinators are in crisis. Without bees, butterflies, and native bugs, we risk losing the food security and biodiversity that sustain us all.

🌱 Why This Matters

Insects in Aotearoa and the world are vanishing. From native bees to butterflies, their decline is a warning sign. Without pollinators, our crops, native bush, birds, and ecosystems all suffer.

We don’t need to wait for government action—every lawn, roadside, and backyard in New Zealand can help.

Just like the UK’s “No Mow May” movement, we can act here too. Nature doesn’t just live in reserves, it lives in our backyards, berms, parks, and road edges. By leaving space for wildflowers and avoiding sprays, we bring life back into our everyday landscapes.

🌍 Simple Actions You Can Take

  • 🚫 Stop mowing during spring and summer flowering times
  • 🌿 Avoid herbicides and pesticides—let native plants thrive
  • 🌸 Plant native wildflowers, shrubs, and trees
  • 🍃 Let parts of your lawn or berm grow naturally
  • 🤝 Join local conservation groups and share the message

Small changes in your garden or street can bring back bees, butterflies, and native insects—helping restore biodiversity and secure our food future.
