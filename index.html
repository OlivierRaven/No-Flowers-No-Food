🌼 No Flowers = No Food 🌼

New Zealand’s insects and pollinators are in crisis. Without bees, butterflies, and native bugs, we risk losing food security, biodiversity, and the health of our cities and countryside.
Action is needed now — but the benefits are huge.

🌱 Why This Matters

Insects in Aotearoa are vanishing. From native bees to butterflies, their decline threatens the plants, birds, and people who depend on them. Pollinators are essential for our crops, orchards, and native ecosystems.

We don’t need to wait for government action — every garden, roadside, and neighbourhood can help bring nature back.

🌍 The Benefits of Being Nature-Inclusive

🌸 Rich Biodiversity

More flowers and wild spaces mean more bees, butterflies, birds, and native life. Every patch counts, even in cities.

🥕 Food Security

Pollinators keep our orchards, gardens, and farms productive. Without them, we lose harvests and nutrition.

🌿 Health & Wellbeing

Nature-rich neighbourhoods improve mental health, reduce stress, and build stronger communities. Learn more.

🌏 Climate & Soil

Less mowing and spraying means healthier soils, cleaner water, and more carbon stored in plants and landscapes.

See also: Nature-inclusive neighbourhoods, and Greener cities for healthier people.

🌼 Actions You Can Take Today

  • 🚫 Stop mowing during spring and summer flowering times
  • 🌿 Avoid herbicides and pesticides — let native plants thrive
  • 🌸 Plant native wildflowers, shrubs, and trees
  • 🍃 Let parts of your lawn or berm grow naturally
  • 🤝 Join local conservation groups and share the message

Small actions in your backyard or street can bring back pollinators, restore biodiversity, and create healthier communities.
